METHODOLOGY

            
How each score and indicator is calculated in this dashboard

            

            

                
RS IBD PERCENTILES

                

                    O'Neil's most fundamental rule: "The stocks that go up the most are those that were already performing the best."
                    The RS Rating ranks every stock 1–99 based on relative price performance. O'Neil found the average RS of great winners before their major moves was 87.
                    Never buy a stock with RS below 70 — you want leaders, not laggards.
                

                

                    
Core framework — identical to IBD/O'Neil relative strength methodology.

                    

                    
Data: Daily closing prices from Firestore (up to 252 sessions, ~1,150 US tickers).

                    

                    
Segments:

                    
  Weekly  →  w1=ret(0,5)  w2=ret(5,10)  w3=ret(10,15)  w4=ret(15,20)

                    
  Monthly →  m1=ret(0,21)  m2=ret(21,42)  …  m12=ret(231,252)

                    

                    
Ranking: rank(x) = (# tickers below) / (n−1) × 100  →  percentile 1–99

                    

                    
Composite scores:

                    
  PCT 1W  = rank(w1)

                    
  PCT 4W  = avg(rank_w1, rank_w2, rank_w3, rank_w4)

                    
  PCT 6M  = avg(rank_m1 … rank_m6)

                    
  PCT 12M = EWM-weighted avg(rank_m1 … rank_m12, factor=0.90)

                    

                    
RS IBD = percentile of  (0.4×rank_3m + 0.2×rank_6m + 0.2×rank_9m + 0.4×rank_12m)

                

            

            

                
VOL-ADJUSTED RS (VA-RS)

                

                    O'Neil insisted on "tight price action" — stocks that consolidate in a narrow range, closing near their highs with small weekly spreads.
                    Wide, loose, erratic structures almost always fail at breakout. The VA-RS captures this quality dimension quantitatively:
                    a stock climbing steadily with shallow pullbacks scores higher than one posting the same return through violent swings.
                    A large positive Δ (VA-RS − RS IBD) signals quality momentum being underestimated by raw RS.
                

                

                    
Concept: Sortino-like ratio — return per unit of DOWNSIDE volatility only.

                    
Upside moves do not penalise a stock. Only drawdown days count in the denominator.

                    

                    
Downside semi-deviation per segment:

                    
  Collect daily log-returns where price fell. Compute their std dev.

                    
  Window = segment × 3 (min 20 days). Capped at available data for m11/m12.

                    

                    
Vol-adjusted ratio:  va_w1 = ret(w1) / semi_dev(days 0–20)

                    
                     va_m1 = ret(m1) / semi_dev(days 0–63)  …  etc.

                    

                    
Composite:

                    
  VA 1W  = rank(va_w1)

                    
  VA 4W  = avg(rank_va_w1 … rank_va_w4)

                    
  VA 6M  = avg(rank_va_m1 … rank_va_m6)

                    
  VA 12M = EWM-weighted avg(rank_va_m1 … rank_va_m12, factor=0.90)

                    
  VA-RS  = percentile(0.4×VA_4W + 0.2×VA_6M + 0.4×VA_12M)

                    

                    
Δ = VA-RS − RS IBD

                    
  Δ > 0 → outperforms on risk-adjusted basis (underestimated by raw RS)

                    
  Δ < 0 → RS inflated by high volatility

                

            

            

                
EMA 10 / 20 — PULLBACKS MA

                

                    O'Neil documented that the greatest winners repeatedly find support at their 10-week moving average during their major advances,
                    offering successive low-risk re-entry opportunities. The bullish MA cascade (EMA20 > SMA50 > SMA200) confirms institutions are in accumulation mode.
                    A pullback within 0.5σ of the EMA10 or EMA20 is the optimal entry zone — reduced risk, trend intact.
                

                

                    
Formula: Standard EMA, k=2/(n+1), seeded on SMA of last n bars.

                    

                    
vs MA20 coloring: Red if > +25% (extended). Neutral otherwise.

                    
vs 52W High:      Red if < −25% (too far from highs). Neutral otherwise.

                    

                    
EMA10 > EMA20 filter: bullish structure.

                    
  Spread = (EMA10/EMA20 − 1) × 100% — cap to avoid overextended setups.

                    

                    
↗ REBOND MA50 (SMA): within last N sessions,

                    
  close ≤ SMA50 × (1+tol%) — touched or broke support

                    
  max 2 consecutive closes below SMA50 (O'Neil undercut-and-recover)

                    
  current close above SMA50 — confirmed bounce

                    

                    
MAX DROP filter: largest 1-day decline in last 20 sessions ≤ N × σ20.

                

            

            

                
BOUNCES MA (SMA 50 / 100 / 200)

                
                

                    SMA 50, 100 and 200 are widely watched by institutions. When a strong stock pulls back to one of these levels, professional buyers who missed the initial breakout use them to accumulate. O'Neil documented how clean touches with light volume and quick recoveries signal institutional demand absorbing supply.
                

                

                    
Within last N sessions (default 10):

                    
  1. Price touched or broke SMA (close ≤ SMA × (1+tol%))

                    
  2. Max consecutive closes below SMA ≤ max_below (default 2)

                    
  3. Current close above SMA — confirmed recovery

                    

                    
SMA = Simple moving average (50, 100, or 200 days — toggle in UI).

                    

                    
Columns:  vs MA = current distance from SMA

                    
          TOUCH = sessions ago (1 = yesterday)

                    
          SOUS MA = consecutive closes below SMA before recovery

                    
          RÉCUP % = return from touch price to today

                

            

            

                
SECTORS — COMPOSITE SCORE

                
                

                    O'Neil's research showed that 37% of a stock's price movement is tied to its industry group. The top 1–2 stocks in the leading group of the leading sector in any market cycle can deliver multiples of the average return. Watching PCT 1W vs PCT 6M divergences reveals which groups are accelerating vs established leaders.
                

                

                    
Eligibility: all 4 percentiles (PCT 1W/4W/6M/12M) ≥ RS MIN (default 50).

                    

                    
Stock score  = avg(PCT 1W, PCT 4W, PCT 6M, PCT 12M)

                    
Sector RS    = avg score of top N stocks

                    
Sector VA    = same using VA 1W/4W/6M/12M percentiles

                    
Sector order = (RS + VA) / 2

                    

                    
Badge RS / VA: computed on ALL sector members — universe breadth signal.

                

            

            

                
SALES ACCELERATION

                
                

                    O'Neil demanded acceleration, not just growth: "In almost every case, earnings growth accelerated sometime in the 10 quarters before a towering price move." Two consecutive quarters of material slowdown is a red flag — regardless of how strong the technical RS may be.
                

                

                    
Source: Google Sheets (Apps Script), consensus estimates.

                    

                    
Columns: LAST QTR % | CUR QTR % | NXT QTR % | SALES 2027

                    

                    
Δ CQ-LQ = CUR QTR % − LAST QTR %   ← acceleration vs prior quarter

                    
Δ NQ-CQ = NXT QTR % − CUR QTR %   ← expected further acceleration

                    
ACC      = count of positive deltas (0 / 1 / 2)

                    

                    
Color: Green ≥ +25%  |  Red < 0%  |  Neutral otherwise

                    
Filter (SALES % ≥ X): requires LAST QTR, CUR QTR and NXT QTR all ≥ X simultaneously.
